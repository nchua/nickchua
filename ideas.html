Gaming

When will gaming companies make good games again? - For years now, gaming companies have been coming out with unfinished games, and expect consumers to download DLC or to continually update their games in order for them to function normally. This has lead to disappointments like the rebooted Star Wars 1 and 2 where players would have to play 40 hours to unlock a single hero (or pay for it). Recently, Bioware (the creators of the beloved Star Wars Knights of the Old Republic and Mass Effect series) released a much anticipated game Anthem. To say that it came with problems is an understatement. There have been issues of completely shutting down game systems, and even crashing systems so badly that they cannot be restarted. These are a few examples resulting from what I believe is a lack of a proper incentive structure for game developers. The pay to win structure in games such as Candy Crush and other mobile apps have showed large developers that the low hanging fruit is in-game purchases. Higher margins (find reference) and lower effort incentivizes this behavior, but where's the incentives for a pure game designer? sure, designer passion may play a role but with large public companies such as EA chasing returns, it seems unlikely that games will ever be released from a gamer-first perspective. So are we doomed to this reality? Here are a few solutions I believe can help combat the problems.
